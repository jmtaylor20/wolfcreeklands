How Land Clearing Jobs Work

The right clearing plan starts with what you want the property to become.

01

Walk the Property

We look at access, terrain, growth, trees, water flow, and the areas you want cleared.

02

Set the Goal

We talk through whether the land is being cleared for building, roads, hunting, farming, or general use.

03

Clear the Area

Brush, overgrowth, small trees, and rough areas are cleared based on the project plan.

04

Prep for Next Step

The property can be shaped, graded, opened for access, or prepared for future work.

Land Clearing Should Make the Next Step Easier.

The best land clearing job opens the right ground, improves access, respects the property, and leaves the site ready for grading, building, roads, food plots, drainage, or other land improvements.

Questions

A few common questions about land clearing, brush removal, homesite clearing, and rural property improvements.

What kind of land clearing do you do?

Wolf Creek Farms clears brush, overgrowth, small trees, trails, homesites, food plots, fire breaks, and access areas.

Can you clear land for a house pad?

Yes. Clearing can be the first step before house pad preparation, grading, fill, and drainage work.

Can you clear hunting property?

Yes. Wolf Creek Farms can clear food plots, trails, fire breaks, shooting lanes, roads, and access areas.

Can you clear driveway or road paths?

Yes. Routes can be opened for driveways, farm roads, hunting roads, and future access roads.

Can you remove all trees?

Project scope depends on the property, equipment access, tree size, cleanup needs, and the final goal. Call to talk through the job.
